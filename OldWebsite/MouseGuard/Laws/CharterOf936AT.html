Charter of 936AT

The First Great Charter of Shogun Wiljoe the Forth of NingyoMordla

In the 936th year after Tobo

In the name of our Honorable Shogun Wiljoe IV of NingyoMordla, we the twelve Daimyo of Hojo by the Grace of the Spirit of Komodoenisis present this sacred and binding charter that holds this under-writtenliberties to be held in our realm of Hojo for ever.

Only those of the line of the Mordla shall henceforth be allowed to be Shogun. They possess the Devine Right to rule over all Hojo by conquest. Those in this offical line shall hence forth be of the name NingyoMordla, with Wiljoe IV being the root of the family lineage. The Shogunate will follow the first born male down the tree. If no males are heirs, then it shall go to the first born female. If no offsprings are heirs, the Shogunate shall go to the heir of the next oldest sibling of the Shogun.

The Royal Family of the Weizhu shall hence forth be the only bloodline that influences the selection of the next emperor. The all non-heir Wei shall be granted governship of their customary homeland of Heiqiuling.

If any mouse who holds chief military service, shall die, and at his death his heir be of full age, he shall have his inheritance by the ancient relief; That is to say the heir or heirs shall have their inheritance without fee. If the heir is not of age, when he comes of age, that is to say, to twenty and one days, he shall have his inheritance without relief and without fine. Until he is of age, his inheritance will remain in the custody of his lords.

The warden of the land of such an heir who is under age, shall not take from the lands of the heir any but reasonable issues, reasonable customs, and reasonable services, and that without destruction and waste of the men or goods. Such damages if they occur will be recovered from the warden once the heir comes of age.

The warden of such an heir must maintain the houses, parks, warrens, ponds, mills, and all other things that belong to the heir without issue. He shall reinstate the heir when he comes of age.

Heirs shall be married without disparagement (not to someone of a lower social status).

A widow, after the death of her lord, shall immediately and without difficulty, have her freedom of marriage and inheritance.

No widow shall be compelled to marry.

Daimyos nor their bailiffs, will seize any land or rent for any debt while a debtor presents sufficient payment of that debt.

The city of Izu shall have all its ancient liberties and customs, as well as land as by water. All other cities and towns shall also have their liberties and customs.

Lawsuits shall be held within the seat of each Daimyo they occur in. Suits between Daimyo shall be held in Edo.

A free mouse shall not be amerced for a small offence, but only according to the degree of said offence. Daimyos may only be amerced by the High Council.

No Daimyo may take the bread or any other goods from any one who is not of the district he presides over without instantly paying money for them.

Measurements for all goods shall be the Izu Standerd

Blood Stones shall be registered with the Izu Registy of Odd and Magically items. The only official research that can be conducted on Blood stones shall be at the above said registry.

We, ratifying and approving these gifts and grants aforesaid, confirm and make strong all the same for us and our heirs perpetually, and by tenour of these presents do renew the same: willing and granting for us and our heirs, that the Charter aforesaid, in all and singular its articles for ever, shall be steadfastly and inviolably observed; and even although any article in the same Charter contained, yet hitherto hath peradventure not been kept. In witness whereof we have caused these our Letters Patents to be made. Witness, Wiljoe IV our shogunate, at Izu, the twelfth day of Fall, in the first year of his reign.

Appended in the 954th year after Tobo

The spirits of Komodoenis, the Almighty Strength of Growth, Kumogataru , the Weaver of the Webs of Unity, and Yogoroth, the Great Consumer shall be the utmost worshiped and only true Major Spirits. Their Pillars of Expansionism, Unity, and Consumption shall create the foundation for a better and brighter Hojo.

The Famu line has hinceforth been restored to their status as a major lineage. Their line shall hinceforth rule over New Famu in the seat of Firestone Mesa. This brings the NingyoMordla Legislation to 13 active members.

Enacted by the Fifth NingyoMordla Legislation.

 

Appended in 966th year after Tobo

The Land of Numa and Tanah Keju shall be officially created and granted represtation in the NingyoMordla Legislative councils. This brings the offical Diamyo Represtatives to ten and five.

The Teritory of Sabaku shall hinceforth be governed by the Himitsu Lineage.

Enacted by the Eighth NingyoMordla Legislation.
